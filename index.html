Overview

The COVID- 19 pandemic across the globe is atypical. The Corona virus has brought the unpredicted fate in the 21st century (United Nations, n.d.). Something that has never happened in the 75-year history of the United Nations. As we all know, we were unprepared for this sort of catastrophic phase. The virus made a serious impact on the human race. People lost their belove ones, struggled for their lives and to sustain. The uninvited guest made our lives miserable, unpredictable, and hopeless in every aspect. We spent our lives for a year or more within our homes. Everything was restricted to online. At the initial stage, there were no protocols or guidelines to face this pandemic leading to disastrous episode. Every single chapter of human life were affected. Medical care, education, travel, food, and beverages and so on were struggling for existence (Impact of COVID-19 on People's Livelihoods, Their Health and Our Food Systems, 2020).

There were times when authorities were uncertain about the cause of admission. Hospitals were flooded with patients, schools and colleges were closed; people were unsure to go out for supplies. The human race was standstill! Even so, during the course of time, most of the countries managed to survive or minimalize the effect of pandemic with the continuous and rigorous efforts learned from similar or minor outbreaks and also by following certain standards and protocols suggested by the health professionals and WHO. The cause, the effect and the remedies were discovered by the scientists across the world. However, the uncertainty and delay in response lead the situation to result in high mortality rates.

The global pandemic is not yet over. We are unsuccessful in completely eradicating the virus from the world. The people and the government are still struggling to defeat the pandemic with a better plan and survive through it without affecting the people and the economy. The experience we acquired through this pandemic is huge. Now, we require a contingency plan which prepares us to go through such outbreaks. It is every evident that the world has to consider the possibility of another outbreak and thus we need to be more vigilant and prepared, given the world has tasted the bitter experience once. Everything in this world should and will play a key role in our preparation against another pandemic. This project is intended to prepare the Fraser Valley to overcome the hurdles and make them to strike against the pandemic with a better and effective plan.

Project Constraints

Scope, Cost and Time

Scope

The scope of this project spreads from researching and understanding the pandemic to construct a functional plan and deliverables which will make the Fraser Valley to defeat another outbreak. A dedicated team will be hired to study and research the whole situation and also to learn how different countries handled and overcome this pandemic globally. Furthermore, surveys will be conducted to know what people in the Fraser Valley thinks about what went wrong, and how this pandemic has affected their lives. Concurrently, based on our experiences we gained through surviving the situation from 2019 will be considered and build a system where we will be ready with enough medical facilities and medical professionals, strategies which will make sure our daily livelihoods are unaffected.

Time

The project has a completion date on Dec 31,2023. All the phases including the implementation of the project is planned to complete on the mentioned date. The whole project will be divided into smaller manageable components with an individual deadline which will be delivered as per schedule. The project breakdown will allow the team members to handle and deliver the project goals more efficiently and will also help the stakeholders to understand the status of the project in a clear manner. The project is calculated to follow spiral model. We will kick start to develop the project deliverables along with the research and survey based on the experience that we gained by surviving the last year. We are expecting changes to be adapted as we get our hands on the reports made by our research team.

Cost

The main investor to this project will be our Canadian Government. The total budget of $ 100 Million is to be funded by federal government of Canada for the proposed project which will be utilizing for the successful running of the project. More investors are expected to join this huge project as it benefits and can make a great change during this pandemic. As more money comes in, the scope of the project can also be expanded. The capital amount will be spent on the research team, website application development and for purchasing the medical facilities. In addition to this, reserving an allowance for unexpected costs during the implementation of the project to be included.




Deliverables

    Mental Models and informational reports


  • Reports will be delivered as mental models as well as informational reports. Mental models can be considered as a map which takes us through someone's thought process on how things should work in the real world, their perception on their acts, consequences, and measures about how solve problems that we encounter("Mental Model," 2021). Informational reports will provide us facts, feedbacks, and other relevant information on the covid impacts. Research team of our project will conduct studies to learn the consequences of this global crisis in all facets of life. The following areas are being considered for the study.


    1. Education
    2. Health care
    3. Small scale and large-scale business
    4. Public transportation
    5. Senior citizens


    • Survey Reports


  • A feedback analysis will be conducted through our website to understand what went wrong, what are the effective measures that made us through this pandemic. Survey will also be conducted at stores and shops, where the consumers can suggest how the public interactions can be improved without causing a catastrophe. Regular analysis of these surveys will be performed, and the report should be submitted to the health officers and the Implementation team for review.


    • Vaccination drive and Awareness


  • Unceasing awareness programs and vaccination drives will be planned in and around Fraser Valley till we reach 100% vaccinated rate. Public should be highly motivated towards the need to vaccination, thus a very key factor to resist the pandemic. Educational Institutions and recreational centres will be advised to provide awareness, proper verification of vaccination. Vaccination Stalls and centres will be launched at easily accessible public places and malls.


    • Website


  • A website application where users like health care authorities and retail stores can record information regarding their necessities and availabilities. Some examples are given below.


    1. Number of beds available/ needed
    2. Number of health care assistance available/required
    3. Requirement of hospital equipment
    4. A daily report of the affected number of personnel and areas in the vicinity. Also, a report of the recovered personnel
    5. Information about test centres and vaccination drives
    6. Retail stores can record their needs through this website. Suppliers would be able to communicate to the stores and can provide goods at reasonable rates. The difference in loss can be sufficed by the government funds to ensure both parties are satisfied



    Based on the requests made through website, arrangements will be made within a short span of time. System will automatically process each request and make reasonable arrangements and solutions which will help us to get through a major chaos. Below activities will be also taken care by our dedicated team after analysing the request.

    1. Hire medical professionals and provide them facilities
    2. Medial equipment availabilities and supplies for hospitals and small-scale practitioners
    3. Advancement of critical care unit and other facilities like ambulances in the Fraser valley and nearby hospitals



    This website will also launch a feature which will act as an online forum to reach out to the government officials for help the people in need. People can raise requests through this forum and can get aid from us.


    Infrastructures


  • Below infrastructures will be made available through this project.


    1. Construction of buildings to accommodate people who suffer from the situation and provide facilities under one roof.
    2. Infrastructure for testing centres in the accessible areas.
    3. Implementing infrastructures for effective social cleanliness without interrupting our daily lives in schools and workplaces.
    4. Providing advance technologies and other facilities to ensure proper education stream through online
    5. Introduce automation wherever possible to reduce human contact. For example, online payments, deliveries being extended to other areas, official matters etc.
    6. Securing availability of daily goods and consumables through agreement with suppliers.


    Awareness programs and training sessions


    Making people aware about the situation we are in and passing the information we have to the society through various digital platforms and if needed, face to face sessions will make sure people know where we stand right now, why government is imposing restrictions on certain things and why authorities are telling us to follow a set of rules. Following awareness programs are planned to execute as part of this project.


    1. Importance of medical health care
    2. Awareness among schools and workplaces about cleaning, disinfecting, and sanitizing their places and surroundings
    3. Conducting training sessions to prepare the local authorities to act up on the pandemic situations more carefully


    Disastrous relief funds


  • Funds will be organized to release emergency backing for below categories.


    1. Loans at relaxed rates to small- scale and large-scale businesses who are struggling because of the pandemic
    2. Monthly allowance to people who lost their jobs due to the pandemic

Project Stakeholders

Project core team


The core team will consist of the project manager, product owners and the team leaders from different modules. This team can be considered as the primary contact for the project. The core team will organize and participate in emergency meetings for problem analysis and decision making.

Supporting staff

The project needs supporting staff to provide a smooth transitioning of the project as well as for continuous effective performance. The team will continue working with the end users to understand and to easily navigate through the implementation without any setback.

Research team

A team which consists of experts from medical science, economists, financial analysts, global experts, and government officials who will be closely working together to study and make reports on the areas where pandemic has affected, the issues and concerns and also some measures that can be considered to overcome such situations based on the study. A group will be constantly monitoring the detection of new variants in the Fraser Valley area and across the world as well.

Project Sponsors

Sponsors will consist of all the potential investors who are willing to support and invest in this project. The vendors can also invest in this project by providing goods and services as venture.

Suppliers/Vendors

All the medical requirements, equipment, and infrastructure facilities required for this project will be obtained through outsourcing. Outsourcing will be handled through our procurement management plan.

People living in Fraser Valley

Fraser Valley is an important stakeholder in this project. This project will not succeed to the fullest without their contribution and willingness to support the system. People should support and use the services provided by us to make this project successful.

End Users

Schools and other educational institutions, small-scale and large-scale industries and hospitals and other health care centres will be considered as the main users of our product and services.

Project Management

    Project Resource Management


  • Before acquiring resources, an estimation procedure will be involved to estimate the resources needed to complete the work. Based on the estimation, skilled individuals will be selected as team members and other resources like hardware and software will be obtained. A dedicated resource control team will be allocated to plan and monitor the facilities and equipment required for our project.


    • Project Communication Management


  • The project team will be formed with professionals who are equally good at technical, communication and interpersonal skills. The following reports and meetings are planned to conduct through out the project life cycle.


    1. Daily status reports will be created and shared by each team among other teams and team leads.
    2. Daily stand-up meetings will be conducted within each team to plan the day ahead and to discuss any impediments the team encounter as well.
    3. Weekly meetings within the core team to track down the progress and for any decision-making events.
    4. Monthly reports will be handed over to the important stakeholders like customers and investors addressing the progress.


    • Project Procurement Management


  • Procurement management will be handled carefully as it involves large amount of scope, time, and cost of our project. The project team will closely work with the suppliers to ensure a smooth procurement process. All the resources coming through external vendors should follow an approval process which goes through the project core team and a quality check process done by our quality control team. Below resources are planned to acquire through outsourcing.


    1. Infrastructure
    2. Medical Equipment
    3. Ambulances


    • Project Risk Management


  • This project holds a high degree of risk factors as it involves human lives, financial stability, and social well-being. Risk analysis will be conducted in a regular interval identifying possible risks and threats which will be deeply monitored by our team. Our risk management team will propose preventive measures to reduce the probability of risk occurrences. Contingency allowances are included in the cost that can be utilized during emergency situations.


    Project Quality Management


  • Quality of the deliverables will not be compromised at any point of time. Devoted team consists of experts in their respective areas will be closely working the development team. Training sessions will be conducted by top health experts for the selected speakers. All the supplies coming through outsourcing are supposed to undergo quality check by our resource control team.


    SWOT Analysis


    Strength: Accessibility of skilled resources in health care, information technology, economy and public administration can be considered as a positive sign towards the success probability of the project. Since we are going through the survival process already, kick starting the project will be way easier since we have enough information and analysis based on the past year experience.

    Weakness: Occurrences of unknown risks are high. For example, virus undergoing mutations forming new variant could change the direction of the project because the situation can demand to execute contingency plans which can put halt on the project. Even though we have skilled medical experts in Fraser Valley, the strength of them shall be questioned. Acquiring the needed resources could be a tough game.

    Opportunity: The completion of this project will be a tremendous victory and will be marked in the global history. Committing this project is giving us a golden opportunity to work with and to get inspired by the field experts. Experience gained through this project is huge and countable. The success of this project could lead to get more commitments with the federal government.

    Threat: Since the project involves some factors that cannot be controlled by humans, some of the risk factors involved with the project is also unknown and unpredicted. Since the project will make an impact on multiple levels of the human life, any minor mistakes could lead to enormous loss. Below risks can be anticipated during the project life cycle.

    1. Detection of new virus variant
    2. Unable to produce enough human and other physical resources as expected
    3. Delays in supplies as there are restrictions on working properly dur to outbreak
    4. Resistant attitude by the people toward the implementation of the project
